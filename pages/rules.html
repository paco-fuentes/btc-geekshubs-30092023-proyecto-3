RULES
Mastermind: Basic Game Rules Objective of the Game: The goal of Mastermind is for the "codemaker" to select a hidden color combination, and the "codebreaker" to try and guess that combination within a limited number of attempts. Initial Setup: The codemaker chooses a secret combination of colors. The combination can consist of multiple different colors, and colors may be repeated. Available Colors: The colors that can be chosen for the combination are typically part of a predefined set, such as red, green, blue, yellow, white, and black, among others. Codebreaker's Attempts: The codebreaker makes an initial guess at the combination. The codemaker provides feedback on the guess. Feedback: Two different colored pegs are usually used to provide feedback. One color indicates if a color is in the combination and in the correct position (correct placement). Another color indicates if a color is in the combination but in an incorrect position (incorrect placement). Iteration: The codebreaker makes successive guesses based on the feedback received. The number of attempts is often limited (e.g., 10 attempts). End of the Game: The game ends when the codebreaker correctly guesses the combination or exhausts the available attempts. Scoring: Performance is evaluated based on the number of attempts used to guess the combination. Variations: There may be variations in the rules depending on versions or preferences, such as the introduction of wildcards, additional constraints, or the option to play in teams. Fair Play: It's important for both parties to play fairly and abide by the agreed-upon rules.
